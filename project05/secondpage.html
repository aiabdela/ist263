Decades ago, as early as 2500bc ancient Egypt, Swimming has been practiced as an enjoyable activity in Greek and Roman civilizations, where the Romans also had it as a required skill for soldiers.
In 776 BC, where competitive swimming gained a place in ancient Greece, and featured as ancient Olympic games that were held in the open water and competing with various strokes.
Adding more years to that phase, which brings us to the Middle age era, where Swimming took a backseat until the Renaissance period. there was a growing interest in physical education.
Competitive swimming as we know it now began in the nineteenth century, with the formation of swimming associations and the running of the first recorded swimming events.
Furthermore, swimming rose to popularity with its inclusion in the modern Olympic Games, which began in 1896.
Technological developments, like as better swimsuit fabrics and underwater cameras, have improved the sport and led to higher performance. Swimming strokes and methods have changed over time, with the freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, and butterfly emerging as the most known styles. It is now a popular activity enjoyed by millions of people all over the world, providing both competitive and leisure opportunities while also increasing physical fitness and general health.
